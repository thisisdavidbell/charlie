Animals are in danger!

Humans are causing lots of climate change.

Climate change is melting ice in the North Pole.

This is making lots of ice drift off into the ocean.

Melting polar ice is also raising sea levels, leading to more frequent and severe floods.

Climate change is also affecting animals in other ways.

Rising sea temperatures and melting ice are reducing the population of krill.

Krill are a main food source for many animals including penguins and whales.

What can you do to help?

There are many things you can do to help protect animals.

  • Recycle paper, plastic, glass, and metal to reduce waste.
  • Plant trees and support reforestation projects to restore habitats.
  • Use energy-efficient appliances and light bulbs to reduce energy consumption.
  • Conserve water by turning off taps when not in use and fixing leaks.
  • Reduce car travel by walking, biking, carpooling, or using public transportation.
  • Support wildlife conservation organizations through donations or volunteering.
